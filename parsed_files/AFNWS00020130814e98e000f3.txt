352 words 14 August 2013 05:44 All Africa AFNWS English Aug 14, 2013 (Voice of America/All Africa Global Media via COMTEX) -- Authorities in Nigeria's Borno state say gunmen have killed at least 56 people in attacks on two small towns near the state capital, Maiduguri
There has been no claim of responsibility but officials suspect the radical Islamist group Boko Haram was behind the attacks
In the biggest attack, gunmen dressed in military uniforms entered the village of Konduga on Sunday and shot dead at least 44 people who were praying at a mosque
A guard at Maiduguri Teaching Hospital said that 26 others wounded in the attack were brought in for treatment
In a separate attack, gunmen entered the village of Ngom on Saturday and killed 12 people
Boko Haram Began in 2002 as non-violent Islamist splinter group Self-proclaimed leader is Abubakar Shekau Boko Haram translates to "Western education is sinful" Based in the northeastern city of Maiduguri Wants Nigeria to adopt strict Islamic law Launched uprising in 2009 Has killed thousands since 2010 Nigerian military intensified crackdown on the group's northeastern stronghold in May Boko Haram has claimed responsibility for many previous attacks in northeastern Nigeria, including some on mosques whose clerics have denounced religious extremism
A new video emerged Monday by Boko Haram's self-declared leader, Abubakar Shekau
In it, he claimed responsibility for several recent attacks, including those in the towns of Bama, Malam Fatori and Gamboru
He also urged Nigerians to abandon the country's constitution and accept Islamic law
Boko Haram has been blamed for thousands of deaths since launching an uprising against the government in 2009
The group said it wa fighting to impose a strict form of Sharia on Nigeria's Muslim-majority north
It's name in the Hausa language means "Western education is sinful." Borno is one of three northeastern states where President Goodluck Jonathan declared a state of emergency and deployed additional troops in May to fight Boko Haram
Rights groups have criticized the military for heavy-handed operations they say have led to hundreds more deaths
