113 words 17 August 2013 11:33 All Africa AFNWS English Aug 17, 2013 (Aswat Masriya/All Africa Global Media via COMTEX) -- Six people were killed and around 50 were injured in clashes that flared on Friday evening between Muslim Brotherhood supporters and army forces in Suez's Arba'een Square
The injuries range from shotgun wounds to cuts, said Abdel Moneim Salem, the Suez General Hospital Director
The Third Field Army Forces managed to break up gatherings for supporters of the Brotherhood and former President Mohamed Mursi after the two parties exchanged fire
The army forces chased the Brotherhood supporters into side streets around Arba'een Square and are scanning the area to prevent their return
